## Apply It This Week

Your homework — do these in the real world:

1. **Audit your own inbox.** Open your email and find one promotional or notification email. Run it through the 8-point red flag checklist. Practice makes it automatic.

2. **Check a sender domain.** Next time you get an email from a company, look carefully at the actual domain (not the display name). You'll start noticing how often they don't match.

3. **Hover, don't click.** Build the habit of hovering over every link to preview the real URL before clicking. On mobile, press and hold.

4. **Teach someone.** Show one family member or coworker the two golden rules. Teaching cements your own learning.

## Level Up — Related Concepts to Explore

Now that you can spot phishing, here's what naturally comes next:

- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Even if you DO get phished, MFA can stop the attacker. Learn how it works.
- **Password Managers:** They auto-fill only on the *real* domain, so they won't enter your password on a fake site — a built-in phishing defense.
- **Business Email Compromise (BEC):** The advanced version of Email #5 — how attackers impersonate executives to trick employees into wiring money.
- **URL Anatomy:** Learn to read a URL properly — subdomains, domains, and how attackers disguise them.
