# 🧪 Adtech247 Mini-Lab #01 — Spot the Phishing Email

**Difficulty:** Beginner · **Time:** ~15 minutes · **Setup required:** None

---

## What You'll Learn

By the end of this lab, you'll be able to:

- Identify the most common red flags in phishing emails
- Understand the psychological tricks attackers use
- Inspect sender addresses, links, and urgency cues like a security analyst
- Apply a repeatable checklist to any suspicious email you receive

No tools. No coding. No signup. Just your eyes and your brain.

---

## Why This Matters

Phishing is the **#1 way businesses get breached.** According to multiple industry reports, the large majority of successful cyberattacks start with a single phishing email. It doesn't matter how strong your firewall is if someone on your team clicks the wrong link.

The good news: once you know what to look for, phishing emails become surprisingly easy to spot. That's what this lab trains.

## The Red Flag Checklist

Before you start, here's the analyst's mental checklist. Use it on every email:

| # | Red Flag | What to Look For |
|---|----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Sender address** | Does the domain actually match the company? Look past the display name. |
| 2 | **Urgency & fear** | "Act now or your account will be closed!" Pressure kills good judgment. |
| 3 | **Generic greetings** | "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name. |
| 4 | **Suspicious links** | Hover before you click. Does the URL match the real site? |
| 5 | **Spelling & grammar** | Professional companies proofread. Attackers often don't. |
| 6 | **Unexpected attachments** | Invoices, receipts, or ZIP files you didn't ask for. |
| 7 | **Requests for credentials** | Legit companies never ask for your password by email. |
| 8 | **Too good to be true** | You won a prize you never entered for. |
